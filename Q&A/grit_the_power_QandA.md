What is the general reception of Angela Duckworth's book "GRIT"? It has received widespread popular acclaim but also significant academic criticism, with reviewers finding the research underdeveloped.

What is Duckworth's core thesis about grit? Grit, defined as passion and perseverance, is more powerful than IQ or talent in predicting life satisfaction and success.

What is a primary criticism of the book's style? It's written as a self-help book, relying on personal stories rather than deep philosophical or extensive research arguments.

How does Duckworth define grit in her book? As a combination of perseverance and passion.

What are the main academic concerns about the "grit" construct? Worries about its construct validity, its self-report measurement, and whether it offers incremental value beyond existing traits like conscientiousness.

Does Duckworth's concept of grit inherently include moral development? No, critics argue it decouples character from moral development, and Duckworth herself admits "gritty villains" are possible.

Do the reviewers believe grit can be excessive? Yes, they argue against Duckworth's dismissal of an "inverse U" for grit, citing examples like obstinacy and loss aversion where too much grit can be detrimental.

What crucial element of character development do the reviewers find missing from Duckworth's book? The concept of "phronesis" or practical wisdom, which orchestrates and integrates various character strengths.

For whom does Duckworth primarily advocate grit education? Underprivileged children, particularly in schools like KIPP, as an anti-poverty tool.

What historical perspective do the reviewers offer regarding grit and poverty? Historically, grit was seen as an antidote to wealth's comforts, not a deficit among the poor, who were presumed to already possess it.

What individualist bias do the reviewers identify in the focus on grit? It risks ignoring external enabling conditions and structural problems like poverty, potentially romanticizing hardship.

How do the reviewers assess the educational advice on grit in the book? They find it under-researched, simplistic, and lacking in nuanced, individualized guidance, relying on qualitative interviews rather than robust data.

Despite their criticisms, do the reviewers find any value in Duckworth's book? Yes, they acknowledge it provides food for thought and highlights the power of determination, particularly appreciating her concluding remarks on grit's limited scope and morality's importance.

What is the reviewers' ultimate concern about the book's impact? Despite a belated caveat, the book largely fuels the misconception that "character" primarily comprises performance virtues like grit, rather than a broader moral concept.
